Architectural Typologies Covered

B

English Baroque

Characterized by dramatic street-level placement, layered classical orders, multi-tiered ornate spires, and circular or semi-circular porticos as exemplified by the designs of Gibbs and Nash.

G

Victorian Gothic Revival

Prominently utilizing rugged stone block walls, soaring pointed arch windows, decorative stone tracery roundels, and picturesque asymmetrical tower layouts.

V

Vernacular Parish Flint

A traditional regional method utilizing split dark flints set into deep mortar beds alongside contrasting limestone ashlar dressings at angles and window frameworks.
