The Journey

A unique slice of London's maritime & community past

Surrey Docks Farm began its journey in 1975 as a scrappy goat farm founded by Hilary Peters on derelict dockland near Greenland Dock. In June 1986, the farm relocated to its current 2.2-acre riverside site at South Wharf. To celebrate the milestone, the animals were walked proudly through the local streets to mark the transition.

Today, operating as a registered charity leasing the land from Southwark Council, Surrey Docks Farm stands as a remarkable urban sanctuary. It remains dedicated to education, farming, and providing a direct connection to nature for Londoners of all ages.

Historical Timeline

The Layers of South Wharf

Long before the farm arrived, this riverside plot hosted shipyard workers, Victorian hospital craft, timber merchants, and brave wartime fire service crews.

1743

The Historic Shipyard

The oldest recorded history of the South Wharf site dates back to a working shipyard. Fascinatingly, a structural fragment of the original 18th-century shipyard brick wall still stands today, forming the farm's northern boundary and anchoring its connection to maritime London.

1883

Metropolitan Asylums Board Receiving Station

Following a stint as a bustling timber wharf, the site was acquired in 1883 to serve as the South Wharf Receiving Station for smallpox and fever patients. Operated by the Metropolitan Asylums Board, patients were brought here before being moved downriver by steamer to isolation hospital ships anchored at Long Reach.

WWII & 1940

WWII Auxiliary Fire Service & Bombing

During the Second World War, the site found crucial utility as a Fire Service river station. However, in 1940, direct bombing completely destroyed the station structures, rendering South Wharf a vast, derelict wasteland for over four decades.

1975

The Seed of Surrey Docks Farm

Pioneered by founder Hilary Peters, Surrey Docks Farm began humble operations on empty land surrounding nearby Greenland Dock. Hilary’s efforts demonstrated that even heavily post-industrial land could support wildlife, crop growth, and livestock husbandry.

1986

The Great Relocation to South Wharf

With the London Docklands Development Corporation reshaping the docks, the farm secured a lease on the historic South Wharf site. The landmark move in June 1986 involved herding sheep, goats, and pigs through local residential avenues in a joyous communal parade.

2020

Award-winning Riverfront Development

The farm entered a bright modern chapter with the completion of its ambitious Riverfront Development. Integrating a new café, multi-purpose educational teaching kitchen, and an extension to the scenic Thames Path, the build was honored with the prestigious Mayor's Prize at the New London Awards.

Uniquely Surrey Docks

Life on the Farm

A look inside the extraordinary residents, local personalities, and unexpected events that shape the colorful tapestry of Surrey Docks.

Diverse Farm Residents

Surrey Docks is proudly home to goats, sheep, pigs, donkeys, and busy hives of honeybees. These animals play a critical role in local educational programs, therapy sessions, and community engagement.

Working Farm Yard

20 Years of On-Site Guardian Life

For two decades (from 1983 to 2003), dedicated education worker Daphne Ferrigan lived on-site in a resident flat with her family, embodying a deep personal commitment to the farm's security, community access, and constant day-to-day welfare.

Daphne Ferrigan Legacy

Digging Up Hidden Treasures

Being positioned on the Thames foreshore, local mudlarking and formal archaeological digs have yielded beautiful fragments of the site's rich past, including handmade 18th-century shipyard nails and Victorian crockery from the fever receiving station.

Milestones & Memoirs

Memorable Moments of Spirit & Grit

Hilary's Literary Vision (1979)

In 1979, the farm's radical founder Hilary Peters compiled her visions, philosophies, and diary entries into her self-published work, "Docklandscape", portraying the raw magic and potential of regenerating industrial riverfront wasteland.

The Piglet Theft Fundraiser (2018)

When a strange piglet theft rocked the community in 2018, locals immediately galvanized behind the farm, launching a major fundraising campaign that greatly boosted site-wide CCTV and modern perimeter security systems.
