Surrey Docks
Farm

A 2.2-acre working city farm grazing on the ruins of a shipyard, a timber wharf, and a Victorian fever hospital — proof that London's docklands never really stop changing hands.

Est. 1975 · Relocated 1986 · Registered Charity

Goats on the wasteland

Surrey Docks Farm began in 1975 as a scrappy goat farm founded by Hilary Peters on derelict dockland near Greenland Dock — scrubland the goats grazed while the closed docks quietly went to seed around them.

In June 1986 the whole operation moved to its present site at South Wharf, the animals led through the streets and along the riverside to mark the occasion. It's a plot with a longer memory than the farm itself: a shipyard recorded as early as 1743, whose original brick wall still forms the farm's northern boundary.

What the site has been

1743

Shipyard

Wooden sailing ships built on the plot; a chunk of the yard's red brick wall survives as the farm's northern boundary today.

1800s

Timber wharf

The shipyard was broken up and the plot became a working timber wharf on the Rotherhithe riverside.

1883–1940

Receiving Station

The Metropolitan Asylums Board's South Wharf Receiving Station transferred smallpox and fever patients by river — then doubled as a WWII Fire Service river station before being bombed in 1940 and left derelict.

1975–86

The Farm arrives

Founded at Greenland Dock, then relocated to South Wharf in 1986 — where it still stands, grazing on all the layers above.

Odd notes from the record

1979

Docklandscape

Founder Hilary Peters wrote a book about the farm's founding years, chronicling goats grazing on scrubland where the docks once stood.

1983–2003

A family on-site

Education worker Daphne Ferrigan lived and worked at the farm with her family for two decades, running workshops like willow weaving.

2018

The piglet theft

Eleven two-week-old piglets were stolen and never recovered — the community rallied to fund better farm security afterward.

2013–17

Foreshore finds

Volunteer digs with the Thames Discovery Programme surfaced shipyard nails and hundreds of shards of Victorian receiving-station crockery.

2020

Riverfront Development

A new events space, teaching kitchen, and an extended stretch of the Thames Path opened — winning the Mayor's Prize at the New London Awards.
