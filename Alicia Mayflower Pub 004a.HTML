Origins as "The Shippe"

The Mayflower Pub stands proudly as one of the oldest riverside pubs in London, boasting a rich history that traces its roots back to approximately 1550. Originally christened The Shippe, this atmospheric tavern served as a vital hub for the rugged sailors, wealthy merchants, and hardworking dock laborers who powered the bustling, chaotic trade of the historic Port of London.

2

The Voyage of 1620

Today, the pub is globally renowned for its deeply-rooted connection to the Mayflower, the historic vessel that transported the Pilgrim Fathers to the New World in 1620.

According to local maritime tradition, Captain Christopher Jones and his crew gathered within these very walls to share drinks, plan, and stock provisions before embarking on their journey. While the Pilgrims ultimately made their final departure from Plymouth, it was here in the quiet waters of Rotherhithe that the Mayflower was moored and fitted for its monumental transatlantic voyage.

"Captain Christopher Jones and members of the crew gathered at the pub before setting sail on their historic voyage..."
3

Destruction & Rebirth

Like much of London's old waterfront, the original timber-framed building succumbed to a devastating fire in the 19th century. However, its story did not end there. In 1951, the pub was meticulously rebuilt, intentionally keeping its traditional riverside aesthetic intact. Today, visitors step back in time as they explore rich wood paneling, historic displays dedicated to the Pilgrim Fathers, and stunning, uninterrupted views across the River Thames.

Did you know?

The Mayflower is the only pub licensed to sell both British postage stamps and international passenger permits, honoring its unique transatlantic heritage.
