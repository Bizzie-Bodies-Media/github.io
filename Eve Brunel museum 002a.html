A Journey Through Engineering History

Nestled in the historic district of Rotherhithe, the Brunel Museum celebrates one of the greatest engineering achievements in history. Located inside the original Brunel Engine House, the museum tells the story of the Thames Tunnel and the brilliant engineers who made it possible.

The museum honours Marc Isambard Brunel and his son, Isambard Kingdom Brunel, whose innovative ideas transformed civil engineering. Visitors can learn how the Thames Tunnel was built beneath the River Thames between 1825 and 1843 despite floods, financial setbacks and dangerous working conditions.

Today, the museum offers interactive exhibits, original artefacts, engineering drawings and guided tours that bring Victorian innovation to life.

Why Visit?

The Brunel Museum offers much more than exhibits. It provides a fascinating journey into Victorian Britain, where innovation and determination transformed the future of engineering.

  • ✅ Learn about the world's first tunnel beneath a navigable river.
  • ✅ Discover the achievements of the Brunel family.
  • ✅ Experience the impressive underground Tunnel Shaft.
  • ✅ Explore original engineering artefacts.
  • ✅ Perfect for families, students and history enthusiasts.
  • ✅ A peaceful hidden gem away from London's busiest attractions.
